Seminar Description

Prerequisite: Most of Undergraduate Abstract Algebra (Group Theory (including group action, Sylow's Theorem), Ring Theory (including polynomial rings and symmetric polynomial), Module Theory, Field Theory (including algebraic extension) and Galois Theory); Basic Linear Algebra, Basic analysis.

This seminar introduces the fundamental structures and techniques of algebraic number theory, with an emphasis on modern applications in cryptography and coding theory. Topics include number fields, ring of integers, ideal factorization, Dedekind domains, class groups, units, and local fields (which was not covered by the previous seminar). We will explore both classical results (e.g. Dirichlet's Unit Theorem, Unique Factorization of Ideals) and (maybe) their implications in modern computational number theory.

By the end of this course, students will have a deep understanding of the algebraic foundations behind number theoretic constructions, and be prepared to read more advanced texts or engage in research-level problems in number theory, arithmetic geometry, and lattice-based cryptography.

The course will combine theoretical exposition with guided exercises in the textbook. It should be noted that one should cover most of the exercises appearing in the textbook.
